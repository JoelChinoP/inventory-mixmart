# Skill - Mixmart Inventory System
Version: 1.1
Optimized for: Codex, implementation agents, and AI coding assistants
Target stack: Next.js App Router, NextAuth.js v4, Prisma ORM 7, Supabase Postgres, Tailwind CSS

## 1. Purpose
Use this skill when implementing, extending, or auditing the Mixmart inventory system.

The goal is to keep future agents aligned with the real business rules, current database shape, and practical constraints of a small or medium store. Do not reinterpret the business from scratch.

## 2. Product Mission
Build an internal inventory system for a small store that sells:

- school supplies
- bazaar items
- snacks
- in-house services
- outsourced services

The system controls products, suppliers, stock entries, stock outputs, current stock, services, reports, users, and roles.

## 3. Expected Agent Behavior
Prioritize:

1. stock accuracy
2. immutable movement traceability
3. simple operational workflows
4. server-side role enforcement
5. consistent cost and profit reports even when purchase costs or suggested sale prices change later
6. Vercel-free-tier efficiency through low query count and server-first rendering

## 4. Current Technical Context
- Next.js App Router is the application framework.
- NextAuth.js v4 is the authentication layer.
- Supabase is used as hosted PostgreSQL, not as the application auth provider.
- Prisma ORM 7 is used for schema, migrations, type-safe access, and Client Extensions.
- The repo uses a reusable soft delete extension for configured catalog/admin models.
- PostgreSQL triggers and constraints enforce stock and cost invariants.
- Tailwind CSS and semantic design tokens define the UI palette.

## 5. Business Sources Of Truth
The business requirements are the source of truth, with these explicit decisions already made:

- Snacks are normal inventory.
- Snacks do not require lots, expiration dates, FEFO, or expiration alerts in this version.
- Purchase cost can change between stock entries for the same product.
- The current product purchase cost is maintained as a weighted-average/reference value in `Product.purchasePrice`.
- Historical cost truth lives on entry items, output items, and stock movements.
- Suggested sale price can change for future sales through `Product.salePrice`.
- Real sale price can differ per sale through `StockOutputItem.unitSalePrice`.
- Suggested sale price history is future scope; do not add `ProductSalePriceHistory` yet.

## 6. Actors
### ADMIN
Can manage products, suppliers, users, entries, outputs, services, reports, and configuration.

Only an `ADMIN` can create other `ADMIN` or `WORKER` users.

### WORKER
Can register stock entries, stock outputs, stock views, and operational services.

Cannot manage users, change critical configuration, or view complete administrative reports.

## 7. Scope
Included:

- products
- suppliers
- product-supplier links
- stock entries and received purchases
- stock outputs for sales, waste, and internal use
- stock movements as immutable ledger records
- services and service supply consumption
- reports
- users, roles, active flags, and soft delete where appropriate

Out of scope unless explicitly requested:

- POS
- cashbox
- invoices or tax documents
- customer CRM
- payments
- full accounting
- multi-branch inventory
- snack lots or expiration tracking
- product sale price history

## 8. Ubiquitous Language
- `Product`: an inventory item.
- `ProductCategory`: `SCHOOL_SUPPLIES`, `BAZAAR`, or `SNACKS`.
- `currentStock`: the fast-read available stock on `Product`.
- `minimumStock`: the low-stock threshold on `Product`.
- `StockEntry`: purchase/order header.
- `StockEntryStatus`: `ORDERED` or `RECEIVED`.
- `StockEntryItem.unitCost`: actual purchase cost for that purchase line.
- `StockOutput`: output header for sale, waste, or internal use.
- `StockOutputReason`: `SALE`, `WASTE`, or `INTERNAL_USE`.
- `StockOutputItem.unitCost`: frozen unit cost used for output valuation.
- `StockOutputItem.suggestedUnitSalePrice`: suggested sale price snapshot at sale time.
- `StockOutputItem.unitSalePrice`: real sale price charged for that line.
- `StockMovement`: immutable stock ledger row.
- `Product.purchasePrice`: current weighted-average/reference purchase cost, not historical truth.
- `Product.salePrice`: current suggested sale price for future sales.
- `ServiceTypeSupply`: simple recipe/BOM for in-house service supplies.
- `ServiceConsumption`: supply consumption generated by a service record.

## 9. Non-Negotiable Invariants
1. Stock must never be negative.
2. A stock output or service consumption must never exceed available stock.
3. `ORDERED` stock entries must not affect stock.
4. `RECEIVED` stock entries must affect stock exactly once.
5. Every stock change must create an immutable `StockMovement`.
6. Historical operational rows must not be physically deleted in normal flows.
7. Products, suppliers, users, product-supplier links, service types, and service supplies may use active flags or soft delete.
8. Reports and user management are admin-only and must be enforced server-side.
9. Critical business rules must be enforced in PostgreSQL constraints/triggers or transaction-safe server services, not only in UI.
10. Cost snapshots must make historical reports independent from later product price edits.
11. Sale price snapshots must make revenue/profit reports independent from later suggested sale price edits.

## 10. Cost Policy
Use weighted-average purchase cost for this business.

When a `StockEntryItem` is received:

```txt
newPurchasePrice =
  ((previousStock * previousPurchasePrice) + (entryQuantity * entryUnitCost))
  / (previousStock + entryQuantity)
```

Rules:

- `StockEntryItem.unitCost` stores the actual cost of the specific purchase.
- `Product.purchasePrice` stores the current weighted-average/reference purchase cost for quick valuation.
- `StockMovement.unitCost` stores the cost used by the movement.
- `StockOutputItem.unitCost` stores the frozen cost for output valuation.
- Reports must not recompute old costs from the current `Product.purchasePrice`.
- If previous stock is zero, the new `Product.purchasePrice` is the incoming `unitCost`.

## 11. Sale Price Policy
- `Product.salePrice` is the current suggested sale price configured by an admin.
- Changing `Product.salePrice` affects only future sales.
- When a sale output is created, copy the current suggested price into `StockOutputItem.suggestedUnitSalePrice`.
- Store the actual charged price in `StockOutputItem.unitSalePrice`.
- Discounts, negotiated prices, and bulk sale adjustments belong in `unitSalePrice`, not in `Product.salePrice`.
- For non-sale outputs (`WASTE`, `INTERNAL_USE`), sale price fields must remain null.
- Revenue and profit reports must use `StockOutputItem.unitSalePrice` and `StockOutputItem.unitCost`.
- Suggested sale price trend/history is future scope; do not implement `ProductSalePriceHistory` in this version.

## 12. Module Rules
### Products
- Required fields: name, category, unit name, minimum stock, purchase price.
- Optional fields: SKU, barcode, description, sale price.
- `currentStock` is materialized for fast reads.
- `purchasePrice` is a current weighted-average/reference cost.
- Products can be deactivated or soft deleted but must remain linkable from historical rows.
- Snacks require no lot or expiration fields.

### Suppliers
- Store RUC, name, phone, contact name, optional address, and notes.
- A supplier can be linked to many products through `ProductSupplier`.
- Do not hard-delete suppliers with history in normal flows.

### Stock Entries
- A stock entry belongs to a supplier and creator.
- It has status `ORDERED` or `RECEIVED`.
- Each item has product, quantity, and `unitCost`.
- `ORDERED` entries do not change stock.
- `RECEIVED` entries increase stock once and create `StockMovement` rows.
- Receipt must update `Product.currentStock` and `Product.purchasePrice` transactionally.

### Stock Outputs
- A stock output belongs to a creator and has reason `SALE`, `WASTE`, or `INTERNAL_USE`.
- Every item must validate available stock inside the same transaction that decrements stock.
- Outputs create `StockMovement` rows.
- Outputs freeze `unitCost` for valuation.
- Sale outputs additionally freeze suggested and actual sale prices.

### Stock
- Low stock is `currentStock <= minimumStock`.
- Out of stock is `currentStock = 0`.
- Stock reads should use indexed product fields and avoid recomputing the full movement ledger.

### Services
- `ServiceType` defines in-house or outsourced service types.
- `ServiceTypeSupply` defines supply consumption per unit for in-house services.
- Non-cancelled in-house service records may create `ServiceConsumption` rows and `StockMovement` rows.
- Outsourced services track workflow status and delivery but should not consume stock unless explicitly configured.
- Service consumption cost is reported from the associated `StockMovement.unitCost`.

### Reports
Minimum reports:

- stock movements by day, week, month, and custom range
- low-stock and out-of-stock products
- product movement history
- service summary by period
- supplier purchase history
- current inventory valuation using `Product.currentStock * Product.purchasePrice`
- output cost by period using `StockOutputItem.unitCost` or `StockMovement.unitCost`
- sale revenue and gross profit using `StockOutputItem.unitSalePrice - StockOutputItem.unitCost`

Reports are admin-only by default.

## 13. Authentication And Authorization
- Use NextAuth.js v4 with JWT sessions.
- Do not use Supabase Auth for app login in this repo.
- The login page must be login-only; no public signup or register link.
- Credentials login must accept `username or email + password`.
- Google login must use `GoogleProvider` without requesting offline access or refresh tokens.
- Google login must only allow existing active users whose verified Google email matches `User.email`.
- Google login must not auto-create users.
- Require `User.isActive = true` and `User.deletedAt = null` for authenticated access.
- Store only password hashes in `User.passwordHash`.
- Do not reveal whether a username/email exists on login failure.
- Enforce role checks in route handlers, server actions, server services, and reports.

## 14. Soft Delete Policy
- Use Prisma Client Extensions, not `prisma.$use`, for soft delete in Prisma 7.
- Soft delete may apply to users, products, suppliers, product-supplier links, service types, and service supplies.
- Do not soft-delete append-only operational history such as `StockEntry`, `StockOutput`, and `StockMovement` unless a later requirement explicitly defines cancellation workflows.
- Keep a raw Prisma client available only for admin/audit/maintenance code that must include deleted rows.

## 15. Performance Rules
The app targets Vercel free-tier style constraints, so each endpoint/server action should aim for 1-3 database round trips.

API/BFF optimization policy for this repo:

- Default to Server Actions for internal web app forms and mutations.
- Use Route Handlers (REST/JSON) only when there is a real consumer: mobile app, third-party integration, webhook, export endpoint, or cross-app reuse.
- Do not scaffold empty `app/api/**/route.ts` files.
- When both Server Actions and Route Handlers exist, both must call shared server services and avoid duplicating business logic.
- For read APIs consumed by the UI, prefer consolidated payloads over many small endpoints to avoid client waterfalls.

Rules:

- Load initial route data in Server Components.
- Avoid client waterfalls for dashboard/catalog/report screens.
- Prefer one shaped Prisma query with `select`/`include` over many small queries.
- Use aggregate SQL or Prisma aggregate/grouping for reports instead of per-row loops.
- Use transaction-scoped writes for stock operations.
- Batch validations whenever possible.
- Cache mostly-read catalog/report data with Next.js cache tags or equivalent project-approved caching.
- Keep stock-changing operations dynamic and transaction-safe.
- Revalidate affected tags/paths after mutations.
- Use a lightweight PWA posture only: manifest/installability and route prefetching first. Do not support offline inventory writes in the MVP.

## 16. Frontend Rules
- Use Server Components for protected pages, list initial data, and reports.
- Use Client Components only for interactive forms, tables, modals, menus, and optimistic UI where justified.
- Keep business rules in server services or database constraints, not client components.
- Use the existing Tailwind palette and semantic tokens.
- Keep operational UI dense, neutral, readable, and accessible.
- Use icons for common toolbar/button actions when available.
- Prefer accessible primitives such as Radix/shadcn for dialogs, dropdowns, tabs, popovers, tooltips, and selects.

## 17. Library Recommendations
Keep:

- `next-auth`: installed and aligned with credentials plus Google OAuth.
- `@prisma/client`, `prisma`, `@prisma/adapter-pg`, `pg`: installed and aligned with Prisma/Postgres.
- `tailwindcss` and `@tailwindcss/postcss`: installed and used for the palette.

Add for the MVP:

- `zod`: runtime validation and inferred TypeScript types for forms, server actions, and route handlers.
- `react-hook-form` plus `@hookform/resolvers`: efficient client forms with Zod integration.
- `lucide-react`: tree-shakable icons for navigation, buttons, tables, and status UI.
- `@radix-ui/react-*` as needed, or generated `shadcn/ui` components: accessible dialogs, dropdowns, tabs, popovers, tooltips, and selects.
- `@tanstack/react-table`: inventory and report tables with sorting, filtering, pagination, and column visibility.

Add only when needed:

- `@tanstack/react-query`: client-side interactive/refetching screens, not basic Server Component pages.
- `serwist` or `@serwist/next`: future service worker/offline shell needs, not current inventory writes.
- `date-fns`: richer date formatting and report ranges.
- `sonner`: consistent lightweight toast feedback.

## 18. Required Tests
- `ORDERED` entries do not change stock.
- `RECEIVED` entries increase stock once.
- Duplicate receive attempts do not duplicate stock.
- Outputs reject insufficient stock.
- Service consumptions reject insufficient stock.
- Purchase cost changes update `Product.purchasePrice` while preserving `StockEntryItem.unitCost`.
- Outputs freeze cost in `StockOutputItem.unitCost` and `StockMovement.unitCost`.
- Sales freeze suggested and actual sale prices.
- Profit reports use sale/output snapshots, not current product prices.
- Soft delete hides configured rows without weakening historical ledger integrity.
- Admin-only screens and mutations reject workers.
- Inactive/deleted users cannot authenticate.

## 19. Acceptance Criteria
An implementation is acceptable when it:

- supports products, suppliers, entries, outputs, stock, services, reports, and users
- protects routes and mutations by role
- uses NextAuth.js login-only authentication
- keeps Supabase as Postgres hosting, not app auth
- has no snack lot, expiration, FEFO, or expiration-alert logic
- handles changing purchase costs without corrupting historical reports
- supports suggested and actual sale price snapshots for sales
- does not implement `ProductSalePriceHistory` yet
- keeps stock non-negative
- records immutable stock movements
- stays within practical endpoint query budgets
- avoids unused/empty REST endpoints; API routes exist only for explicit use cases
